What Is BBAN?

Built to Bridge the Gap

The Benue Business Angels Network (BBAN) is an initiative designed to support the growth and development of nano and micro businesses, as well as startups in Benue State.

Inspired by popular business shows like Shark Tank and Lions' Den, BBAN bridges the gap between early-stage entrepreneurs and investors — providing mentorship, technical support, and funding.

The network also produces a televised series where entrepreneurs pitch live to the angels, showcasing the entrepreneurial spirit of Benue State for the world to see.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply to pitch at BBAN?

BBAN is open to entrepreneurs and startup founders based in Benue State or with significant operations in Benue. Your business can be at the nano, micro, or early startup stage. We welcome applications from all sectors — tech, agriculture, health, education, fashion, food, and more.

How much funding can I receive from BBAN?

Funding amounts vary by deal and are determined through negotiation between the entrepreneur and the angel(s). In past seasons, investments have ranged from ₦500,000 to over ₦5 million. Angels may offer equity stakes, convertible notes, or revenue-share arrangements.

Does BBAN take equity from all applicants?

No. Only startups that successfully negotiate and close a deal with an angel will have equity involved. The specific terms — including equity percentage, funding amount, and repayment conditions — are negotiated individually between the entrepreneur and the angel.

What is the application process like?

You submit an online application with a description of your business, your funding ask, and supporting materials. Our selection team reviews all applications and shortlists the strongest candidates. Shortlisted founders are then invited to Hub 17 for a coaching session, followed by the live pitch event.

How do I become a BBAN Angel?

Interested angels complete a membership application. There is an annual membership fee that grants access to BBAN's deal flow, exclusive events, co-investment opportunities, and the BBAN community. Angels are vetted to ensure they bring genuine value beyond capital — including mentorship, networks, and expertise.

Is the pitch event televised?

Yes! BBAN pitch sessions are filmed for broadcast on local television and distributed online through Hub 17's YouTube channel and social media platforms. This gives entrepreneurs significant media exposure even if they don't close a deal on the night.

Can I apply even if my business is very early stage?

Absolutely. BBAN is specifically designed to support nano and micro businesses as well as early-stage startups. You don't need a fully built product or revenue to apply — a clear idea, a compelling problem, and a credible team are what matter most at this stage.
